Scout-Class Drone

The eyes and ears of the swarm. Scouts are lightly armored, highly maneuverable drones tasked with prospecting the asteroid and identifying high-yield ore veins before the heavy machinery arrives.

LIDAR Primary Sensor
80 m/s Max Velocity
Zero Payload Capacity

Deep-Scan Prospecting

Scouts do not dig. Instead, they fly in synchronized grid formations over the asteroid's surface, using Ground-Penetrating Radar (GPR) and X-ray fluorescence spectrometers to look beneath the dust.

As they scan, they continuously beam data back to the Queen (Mothership), which compiles a real-time, high-resolution 3D topographical and chemical map of the asteroid. Once a dense pocket of platinum or water ice is located, the Queen generates a waypoint and dispatches the Diggers.
